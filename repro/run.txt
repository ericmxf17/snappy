  FINDING 3: Reads randomly stall for 20-30 seconds
==========================================================================

  Six cold rounds. Watch WHERE the stall lands: it moves between balance and
  positions, and between accounts. It is not one slow endpoint, and it is not
  one slow account — but the frequency differs enormously between accounts.

    acct1: balance=   0.3s  positions=   0.3s  |  acct2: balance=   0.3s  positions=   0.3s
    acct1: balance=   0.2s  positions=   0.4s  |  acct2: balance=   0.3s  positions=   0.4s
    acct1: balance=  22.3s  positions=   0.4s  |  acct2: balance=   1.4s  positions=   0.4s
    acct1: balance=   0.2s  positions=  30.6s  |  acct2: balance=   0.3s  positions=   0.3s
    acct1: balance=   2.3s  positions=   1.4s  |  acct2: balance=   0.2s  positions=   0.4s
    acct1: balance=   0.2s  positions=  22.6s  |  acct2: balance=   0.2s  positions=   1.6s

    acct1 (...WR20):  median  12.3s   worst  30.6s
    acct2 (...8AUQ):  median   0.4s   worst   1.6s

  A balance call returns cash. It does not read positions. So a 25s balance call
  cannot be explained by how many holdings the account has.

  For a voice assistant this is not slow, it is BROKEN: a 27-second pause after a
  spoken question is indistinguishable from a hang. And caching cannot help — a
  COLD read is precisely what stalls.
